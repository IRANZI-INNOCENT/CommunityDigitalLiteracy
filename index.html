Community Initiative for Digital Literacy

Website Development Plan Bridging the Digital Divide in Kenya

100+ Students Impacted Girls in STEM Rural Outreach Community Driven

1. Purpose & Goals

  • Showcase Impact: Highlight 100+ students trained, girls’ participation in STEM, and notable media features.
  • Engage Stakeholders: Attract volunteers, donors, and partners to support digital inclusion.
  • Educate & Inspire: Share transformative stories and the vital importance of digital literacy.
  • Support Sustainability: Drive ongoing updates, fundraising, and active community involvement.
Primary Audiences: Local communities, partners, donors, volunteers, media, educators.

2. Website Structure

  • Home: Mission, hero image, impact stats, Calls-to-Action (Donate, Volunteer)
  • About: Digital divide context, founder story, project overview
  • Impact: Metrics, student stories, gallery, media links
  • Programs: Curriculum, schedule, digital hubs, girls in STEM
  • Get Involved: Volunteer sign-up, donation, partnership form
  • Blog/News: Updates, spotlights, challenges
  • Contact: Form, email, address, social links

3. Design & User Experience

Colors

  • Green (Primary)
  • Red
  • Black
  • White/Gray (accent)

Typography

Open Sans & Roboto via Google Fonts

Icons

Font Awesome for scalable, modern icons.

Imagery

High quality authentic photos of students, volunteers, training (stock/placeholder for now).

Layout

Responsive Bootstrap (Tailwind) grid, sticky navbar/footer, cards for CTAs/stories.

UX Requirements:
  • Fast load, optimized images, minified assets
  • Intuitive navigation, alt text, keyboard support
  • Subtle hover animations

4. Functionality & Features

  • Static Content: Homepage, Program, About, Impact via Django templates
  • Dynamic Features:
    • Contact form with email notifications
    • Donation integration (M-Pesa, PayPal)
    • Volunteer sign up (with database)
    • Blog system with categories and search
    • Newsletter sign-up (Mailchimp)
  • Interactive: Carousel, modals, gallery, filters via JS/Bootstrap
  • SEO/Analytics: Meta tags, keywords, Google Analytics, mobile friendly

5. Development Timeline

Month 1
Wireframes, tech stack, Django setup
Month 2
Core pages, Bootstrap styling, basic functionality
Month 3
Dynamic features, SEO, testing
Month 4
Deploy, analytics, launch

6. Technical Stack & Sustainability

  • Backend: Django, SQLite (dev), PostgreSQL (prod), models for Blog/Contact/Volunteers
  • Frontend: Django templates, Bootstrap (via Tailwind), custom CSS, Vanilla JS
  • Hosting: Heroku, PythonAnywhere, DigitalOcean, domain (.org), SSL (Let’s Encrypt)
  • Maintenance: Admin panel for updates, regular backups, monitoring
  • Cost Optimization: Free tiers, Google Analytics, scalable design, community feedback (Swahili translation, mobile-first)

7. Measuring Success

  • Traffic metrics – target: 100+ monthly visitors (first 3 months)
  • Engagement – time-on-page for Impact/Get Involved
  • Conversions – sign-ups, form submissions, donations
  • SEO – Google keyword rankings, mobile performance
  • Community feedback – from partners, users, and local leaders

8. Next Steps

  • Sketch wireframes for main pages (Home, Impact, Blog)
  • Curate and organize existing content (photos, media, student stories)
  • Research and select optimal domain and hosting provider
  • Gather feedback from community & partners on prototype
  • Iteratively build and launch the MVP website
